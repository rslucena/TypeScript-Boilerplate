# Branding: The Praxis Framework

## Naming Concept: "Praxis"
"Praxis" represents the process by which a theory, lesson, or skill is enacted. In software engineering, we often spend too much time theorizing about architecture and too little time building. Praxis is the application of solid engineering principles into actual, high-performance code. **Less magic, more engineering.**

## Visual Identity & The Arrow

The logo of the Praxis framework focuses entirely on a highly stylized typography.

### The Arrow Element
The core differentiator of the logo is the **Arrow** integrated directly into the typography (often within the 'P' or cutting across the structure). This is not just an aesthetic choice, but a representation of the framework's core values:

1. **Upward Momentum (Growth):** The arrow pointing upwards to the right represents scalability and business growth. The boilerplate is designed to not just start projects, but to scale them to the Enterprise level without hitting performance bottlenecks.
2. **Speed (The Bun Runtime):** The sharp edges of the arrow symbolize cutting-edge speed. Because Praxis is built on the **Bun** runtime and utilizes highly optimized builds (`tsup`), the arrow is a visual nod to the extreme velocity at which the system operates.
3. **Bypassing the Boilerplate:** The arrow visually "cuts through" the structure, representing how the `bun gen:domain` CLI cuts through days of tedious setup. It takes you directly to the business logic, skipping the mundane boilerplate setup.

### Typography
The geometric and bold structure of the letters conveys **solidity and type-safety** (Zod + Drizzle). It is a framework you can trust for banking, fintech, and enterprise-grade applications.
